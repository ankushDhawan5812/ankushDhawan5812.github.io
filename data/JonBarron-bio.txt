Ankush K. Dhawan is a robotics researcher and engineer focusing on electromechanical systems, tactile sensing, and manipulation. His experience spans embedded hardware design, firmware, and perception. Recent work includes DenseTact‑Mini for dexterous grasping, transformer‑based cloth layer detection with DenseTact 2.0 (IROS 2025), and SPRoUT vine robots for urban search and rescue (SSRR 2024). He has contributed to AI‑enabled consumer hardware at Opal Camera and helps teach CS123 at Stanford, where he is a Robotics PhD student.
